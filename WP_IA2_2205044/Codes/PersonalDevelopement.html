“Twelve Pillars” by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener

  • Chapter 4

    In this chapter we see Michael deciding to take action by putting more time in the growth of his relationship with his wif, Amy. We could see him openly sharing his frustrations about not having a clear starting point in his pursuit of success which is something majority of us can relate to. The fourth pillar highlights the value of jotting down goals, showing how this act breathes life into them and how the journey towards achieving these goals are often more enriching than reaching the goals themselves.

  • Chapter 5

    The fifth pillar is centered aon efficient time management, stressing the importance of carefully allocating time towars the endeavors corresponding to one's objectives. It also highlights the contrast of regret and discipline, indicating one's actions today shape their future selves.

  • Chapter 6

    This chapter uses Michael to showcase why it is important to Surround Yourself with the Best set of People as Michael realizes the influence of his friends on his life and goals. The sixth pillar emphasizes the the importance of surrounding oneself with successful, influential individuals who can shape one's attitude, actions, and future. It showcaeses the power of association and encourages evaluating relationships to ensure they align with personal growth.

  • Chapter 7

    The seventh pillar stresses the significance of self-education and continuous learning throughout life. It emphasizes reading books and learning from successful people as vital tools for personal growth and development. The chapter concludes with the importance of self-education as a foundation for success and lifelong enrichment.
